What Supernova is

Most chains make you wait. Supernova doesn't.

A blockchain normally bundles two jobs together: agreeing on what happens, then running it. Supernova splits them apart. The network agrees on the order of transactions first and executes them right after, so it moves at the speed of agreement instead of computation. The result you feel in here: blocks every 600 milliseconds (ten times faster than before), and a transaction that is final in 100 to 250 milliseconds, faster than you can blink.

Clawbackshard 2

The attacks that drain you elsewhere bounce here

On Ethereum and other chains where the block builder freely orders transactions, they can slide bots around your trade or jump the queue ahead of it. That is where sandwiches and MEV live. MultiversX resists it at the protocol level: the order of transactions in a block follows a deterministic rule seeded by protocol randomness, verified in consensus, so a proposer can't reorder them to front-run or sandwich you. Sub-second finality leaves almost no window to try. Native guardians (onchain 2FA) then block drainers even if someone gets your secret phrase.

But how is it free

No wallet. No gas. Still real.

The magic trick has three honest parts.

1A key is born in your browser. No wallet, no seed phrase, no app. It exists for your session and signs your moves.
2A relayer pays the gas. Your signed transaction is forwarded by a relayer that covers the tiny testnet fee, so you pay nothing and still land a real transaction.
3Your score is just your onchain activity. One move is one transaction, recorded on the chain for anyone to verify. No hidden server, no made-up number. The chain is the scoreboard.
