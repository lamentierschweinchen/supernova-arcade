Two modes, one game

Red cards are incoming attacks draining a valueless game token. Tap one to fire a real gasless counter-transaction that claws the amount back before its settle window closes. Green ones are inflows; leave them (recall one by mistake and you forfeit it). In ETHperience mode, attacks pour in fast and mostly land, the way wallet drainers, sandwiches, and MEV play out on Ethereum today. Switch on a MultiversX Guardian and a native co-signer auto-blocks the same attacks before they settle, so your money stays safe with little effort.

The guardian is only half of it. On MultiversX a block proposer can't reorder your transactions to sandwich or front-run you in the first place: the order is fixed by a deterministic rule seeded by protocol randomness, verified in consensus. The reordering those attacks rely on isn't on the table here.

ETHperience is a themed hard mode. Both modes run on MultiversX testnet (real onchain, gasless). This game never runs on Ethereum. The round seed and your final balance are computed onchain, so the score is real and anyone can verify it, and every run is unique.
